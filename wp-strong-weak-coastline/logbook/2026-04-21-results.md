## 2. Principal findings

### 2.1 Under per-cell Ω recalibration, the (N, δt/T_m) plane collapses to one axis

For every (α, δt/T_m) slice, V varies by ≲ 0.005 across N ∈ {3, 6, …, 96}
(excluding N=3 drive-LD-breach rows, where V still agrees at the third
decimal). The coastline is therefore a **1D curve in δt/T_m**, not a
2D diagram, when Ω is recalibrated per cell. This is a stronger claim
than the memo §3 anticipated and reframes the scope: N carries no
independent physics under the chosen calibration.

This does *not* contradict the memo's choice to map both axes — the
flatness itself is the result, and it is only visible because we
mapped both axes. The option-b control slice (see §2.4 below) confirms
that in a fixed-Ω protocol, N is decisive.

### 2.2 P ≈ 1 across the grid — Doppler-merging regime not reached

Except at the N=3 drive-LD-breach row, P saturates near 1.00 for all
(N, δt/T_m, |α|) cells. By the §4.2 rubric, the weak-binding cells we
observe are therefore all in the **pulse-broadening** regime
(V low, P high), never in the **Doppler-merging** regime
(V low, P low). The δ = 0.5 · ω_m detuning used for the P observable
sits between the 0th and 1st motional sidebands, which a coherent state
with ⟨n⟩ = 9 does not visibly broaden into. Reaching Doppler-merging
would require either a smaller |δ| (resolving teeth locally) or a
larger η|α| than this grid accesses.

### 2.3 α-dependence is non-monotonic

At δt/T_m = 0.80 (the coastline corner):

| |α| | V | δ⟨n⟩ peak | comment |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 0.998 | ~0 | strong-binding preserved; vacuum has no Doppler width |
| 1 | 0.760 | small | onset of pulse-broadening |
| 3 | 0.101 | — | deep into pulse-broadening; WP-V regime |
| 5 | 0.377 | — | **partial recovery** — unexpected |

The α=5 → V=0.38 recovery from the α=3 → V=0.10 trough is the most
surprising finding of this pass. Provisional explanations: higher-order
Debye–Waller structure; Jaynes–Cummings-like revival intrinsic to the
engine at large η√⟨n⟩; or genuine physics of the
motional-LD-breaching regime. *This is exactly the kind of residual
structure that §4.5 of the README names — falsification of the
χ-collapse is accompanied by substantive structure, not noise.*

### 2.4 χ-collapse is falsified (a positive result per §4.5)

See [coastline_chi_collapse.png](../plots/coastline_chi_collapse.png).
Cells at similar χ carry V values differing by O(1). For instance at
χ ≈ 1.2, V spans from 0.10 (α=3) to 0.69 (α=5) to 0.85 (α=1). The
reference Gaussian V = 1 − exp(−χ²) passes through the middle of the
point cloud but tracks no individual α row. The data **refute** the
root-sum-square composition hypothesis of the memo §3.4.

What remains true: each individual width scale does act monotonically
on V within its own row (narrow δt helps; small α helps). The failure
is specifically that the three widths do *not* combine in quadrature.

### 2.5 Control slice (option b, fixed Ω = Ω_Hasse) recovers expected Rabi-node structure

See [coastline_secondary.png](../plots/coastline_secondary.png) right
column. At δt/T_m = 0.13 with Ω held at Ω_Hasse, V sweeps through the
N-pulse Rabi envelope:

| N | N·Ω_eff·δt / (π/2) | V (|α|=3) |
|---|---|---|
|  3 | 0.10 | 0.01 |
|  6 | 0.20 | 0.04 |
| 12 | 0.40 | 0.14 |
| 24 | 0.80 | 0.53 |
| 48 | 1.60 | 0.62 |
| 96 | 3.20 | 0.96 |

The non-monotonicity at 1.60 (near Rabi-envelope node 2·π/2 → zero
diamond, but here still high because net rotation 1.6·π/2 ≈ 2.5 rad is
*not* a full node) and the recovery to 0.96 at net_rot = 3.2·π/2 ≈ 5
rad confirm the Guardian-2 D9 caveat: features in option-b space are
power-broadening artefacts, not coastline features. This is exactly
why option (a) was chosen as the primary in §4.1 of the memo.

## 4. Open questions / follow-ups

1. **α=5 V-recovery mechanism (§2.3).** Physics vs engine artefact is
   ambiguous. A minimal probe: extend the |α| sweep to {3.5, 4.0, 4.5, 5.5}
   at δt/T_m = 0.80 and report V(|α|). If the recovery is monotone in
   |α| above 3, it is physics. If it oscillates, it is likely a finite-N
   revival we should name.
2. **Doppler-merging regime not reached.** Either the P-observable
   detuning should be tuned per-cell to sit inside the broadened
   sideband envelope (δ = (ηα)·ω_m would land at the 1-σ Doppler
   halfwidth) or we should widen the |α| axis beyond 5. Flagged for
   WP-C v0.2 if the council wants the (V low, P low) quadrant explicitly
   characterised.
3. **N-independence as lemma.** The observed flatness V(N) ≈ const
   under option (a) merits a one-paragraph analytic note: at per-cell
   π/2 calibration, N and δt enter the pulse phase only through their
   product, so at fixed product the train-length axis is degenerate
   modulo intra-pulse motion. The residual intra-pulse-motion
   contribution is what the (negligibly small) 0.005-level spread
   measures.
4. **Floquet-synchronisation (§8 of memo).** The v0.1 data show no
   structure in the V or P maps that tracks N-phase rather than
   N-bandwidth (V(N) ≈ const). Per memo §8 promotion criterion, this is
   **insufficient evidence for promoting the Floquet framing** to the
   Discussion level. Retain as commentary; revisit if v0.2 finds
   N-phase-tracking structure.

## 6. Patch notes (v0.1.1, 2026-04-21)

Review response to four findings flagged against v0.1. No change to the
underlying numerical data in
[coastline_v1.h5](../numerics/coastline_v1.h5); all fixes are labelling,
contract, and rendering.

### 6.1 ω_m unit convention (High, finding 1)

The prose in README §3 and kickoff §1 named ω_m/(2π) = 1.3 MHz as the
anchor. The engine convention
([scripts/stroboscopic_sweep.py:51](../../scripts/stroboscopic_sweep.py#L51))
is `omega_m = 1.3` **angular** rad per engine time unit — so
ω_m/(2π) ≈ 0.207 cycles per engine time unit, and Hasse's physical
1.3 MHz is recovered only under the specific choice of engine time unit
= 2π μs. The dimensionless dynamics depend only on η, Ω/ω_m, N, and
t_sep_factor, so none of the observations in §§1–5 above change — but
the wording was imprecise.

Fixes:
- README §3 rewritten to explain the convention explicitly and to note
  that all per-cell reporting is dimensionless (Ω_eff/ω_m).
- Kickoff §1 parameter table relabels the ω_m row as "angular, engine
  units".
- Driver's startup print now shows ω_m in rad per engine time unit and
  cross-refs the engine convention docstring.

### 6.2 HDF5 schema drift (Medium, finding 2)

README §5 bullet 4 and the driver's module docstring named validity
diagnostics as "per-cell h5 attributes" and implied a top-level
`/control_fixed_omega_hasse`. The driver writes them as (nN, ndt)
**datasets** under each `/alpha_{X}pY/` group, with the control slice
nested at `/alpha_{X}pY/control_fixed_omega_hasse/`.

Fixes:
- README §5 bullet 4 now spells out the actual on-disk schema: arrays
  as datasets (because they vary per (N, δt/T_m) cell, not per file),
  control nested per-|α| (because the slice observables depend on |α|).
- README §§2.2 and 2.3 sentences about "dataset attribute" and
  "per-cell h5 attributes" corrected to name `fock_leakage_top5`,
  `ld_flag_drive`, and `ld_flag_motional` as datasets.
- Driver docstring block rewritten as a full schema description.

### 6.3 Motional-LD hatching was being suppressed (Medium, finding 3)

Plot routine previously used `d['ld_mot'] & ~d['ld_drive']` so that
cells breaching both flags showed only the drive-LD hatch. For
|α| ∈ {3, 5} every cell breaches motional-LD, and 10 cells per |α|
also breach drive-LD, so the overlap set is load-bearing (10 cells × 2
|α| values = 20 under-described cells). Secondary plot did not read
`ld_flag_motional` at all.

Fixes in
[plot_coastline.py](../numerics/plot_coastline.py):
- Suppression removed; drive-LD (white `///`) and motional-LD
  (red `···`) hatches now render as independent, layered overlays on
  both the primary V/P maps and the secondary diamond + |δ⟨n⟩| panels.
- Legend on the primary figure updated to state explicitly that cells
  breaching both carry both hatches.

### 6.4 Impulsive-limit overlay (Low, finding 4)

Memo §4.5 specifies the impulsive overlay as an analytic reference line
on the δt/T_m → 0 edge of the primary heatmaps. The v0.1 secondary
plot lacked it entirely.

Fix: `plot_coastline.py` now uses
[`build_impulsive_train`](../../scripts/stroboscopic/sequences.py#L80)
to compute V in the exact impulsive-kick limit (gap = T_m, pulse_area
= π/(2N) to match the recalibrated-Ω net train rotation) and overlays
it at the left edge of each V heatmap per |α|.

**Observation from the overlay.** V_impulsive is essentially uniform:
**V ≈ 0.865 across all N ∈ {3, 6, 12, 24, 48, 96} and all
|α| ∈ {0, 1, 3, 5}.** This is a stronger reference than the memo
anticipated: the impulsive limit gives an N- and α-independent
coherence floor set by the Debye–Waller factor alone. The coastline
therefore measures the **δt-dependent departure** from that floor, and
the α-dependence of the finite-δt V (§2.3 above) is a width effect of
finite pulse duration, not something inherent to the impulsive
reference. This tightens the §2.3 finding: V(α=5) > V(α=3) at
δt/T_m = 0.80 is genuinely a finite-δt phenomenon (both approach the
same impulsive limit), not a sign that the impulsive limit itself is
non-monotonic.
