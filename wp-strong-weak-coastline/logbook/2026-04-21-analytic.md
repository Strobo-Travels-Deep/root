## 1. Lemma A — Motional heterodyne at $t_\text{sep} = T_m$

**Statement.** The gap propagator between stroboscopic pulses, at
$\delta = 0$ and $t_\text{gap} = T_m = 2\pi/\omega_m$, is the
identity on the Fock basis. The full train propagator reduces to
$U_\text{train} = K^N$ with no gap-mediated accumulation of motional
phase.

**Proof.** $U_\text{gap}|_\text{mot} = \mathrm{diag}(e^{-i\omega_m n
t})$, and at $t = T_m$ every eigenphase is $-2\pi n$ for integer $n$,
i.e. $e^{-2\pi i n} = 1$. Identity. ∎

**Numerical confirmation.** Build $U_\text{gap}$ for $\text{NMAX}=80$
and measure $\max|U_\text{gap} - \mathbb{1}|$. Result: **$1.1\times
10^{-13}$**, machine precision.

**Consequence.** At $\delta = 0$ the entire $\alpha$-dependence of $V$
lives in the *intra-pulse* structure of $K$; gaps contribute nothing.
At $\delta \ne 0$ the gap carries only a spin-frame phase
$e^{\pm i \delta T_m/2}$ that is $\alpha$-independent. This explains
the v0.1.1 Doppler probe's empty (V low, P low) quadrant: there is
nothing in the gap that can Doppler-broaden.

## 2. Lemma B — Impulsive-limit $V$ floor

**Statement.** In the exact impulsive limit $\delta t \to 0$ with
$N \cdot A = \pi/2$ and $t_\text{sep} = T_m$, the tooth visibility
for a coherent state $|\alpha e^{i\vartheta_0}\rangle$ under the
option-(a) calibration is

$$V_\text{imp} = \tfrac{1}{2}(1 + e^{-2\eta^2}),$$

independently of $\alpha$ and $N$, whenever
$|\alpha| \geq \pi/(4\eta)$. For $\eta = 0.397$, the threshold is
$|\alpha| \approx 1.98$ and the floor is **$V_\text{imp} = 0.86482$**.

**Derivation sketch.** In the impulsive limit intra-pulse motion is
frozen, so the per-kick unitary is
$K = \cos(A/2)\mathbb{1} - i\sin(A/2) \Sigma$ where
$\Sigma = (\begin{smallmatrix}0 & C\\C^\dagger & 0\end{smallmatrix})$
and $\Sigma^2 = \mathbb{1}$. Under Lemma A, $U_\text{train} = K^N$.
At fixed $NA = \pi/2$ and $A \to 0$, the train propagator is
$U = \tfrac{1}{\sqrt 2}(\mathbb{1} - i\Sigma)$. Applying to the
initial spin coherence $\tfrac{1}{\sqrt 2}(|\!\downarrow\rangle + i|\!\uparrow\rangle)
\otimes |\alpha e^{i\vartheta_0}\rangle$ and projecting onto
$\sigma_x, \sigma_y$ uses only the coherent-state overlap identity
$\langle\beta|\beta'\rangle = \exp(-\tfrac{1}{2}(|\beta|^2+|\beta'|^2) + \beta^*\beta')$
and gives

$$
|C|(\vartheta_0) = \tfrac{1}{2}\sqrt{1 - 2e^{-2\eta^2}\cos(4\eta\alpha\cos\vartheta_0) + e^{-4\eta^2}}.
$$

Minimising over $\vartheta_0$ when the cosine argument spans a full
period gives $\min|C| = \tfrac{1}{2}(1 - e^{-2\eta^2})$ and hence
$V_\text{imp} = \tfrac{1}{2}(1 + e^{-2\eta^2})$. Full derivation in
[notes/analytic-reference.md §3](../notes/analytic-reference.md). ∎

**Numerical confirmations.**

| check | deviation | verdict |
|---|---|---|
| closed-form $\min|C|$ formula vs $\tfrac{1}{2}(1 - e^{-2\eta^2})$ saturation, $|\alpha| \in \{2, 3, 5, 8\}$ | **0.0** (exact) | PASS |
| $V_\text{analytic} = 0.86482$ vs numerical impulsive overlay from `compute_impulsive_V`, $|\alpha| = 3$, $N \in \{3,6,12,24,48,96\}$ | **4.4 × 10⁻¹⁶** | PASS |

All three probes of the earlier numerics (`coastline_v1.h5`
impulsive column, α-recovery v1/v2 reference line, Doppler probe)
are consistent with $V_\text{imp} = 0.86482$ to the precision at
which they were reported (three decimals).

## 3. What this resolves and what remains

**Resolved by Lemma A.**
- Memo §9.1 is now a lemma, not just a numerical finding. The
  (V low, P low) quadrant is unreachable not because the grid wasn't
  dense enough but because $U_\text{gap} = \mathbb{1}$ at $t_\text{sep}
  = T_m$ makes Doppler amplification impossible in principle.
- The v0.1 observation that $V(N) \approx$ const under option-(a) has
  a one-line explanation: $U_\text{train} = K^N$ and $K$ has no
  independent $N$-dependence beyond the pulse area $A = \pi/(2N)$, which
  enters only through the combination $N\cdot A$ pinned to $\pi/2$.

**Resolved by Lemma B.**
- The impulsive-limit overlay value is now derived, not just
  numerical. V_imp = 0.86482 at $\eta = 0.397$, exact.
- The α-recovery v2 Test A finding ($P = 1$ at the V minimum) is
  now structural: at finite $\delta t$ the departure from Lemma B
  is the V oscillation, but the *coherence* amplitude is preserved
  because the off-tooth sidebands are α-independent by Lemma A.
- Memo §5.3 attribution is narrowed: the V($|\alpha|$) oscillation at
  finite $\delta t$ is unambiguously a **finite-$\delta t$
  correction** to the impulsive lemma. JC-style revival (hypothesis
  (i)) cannot contribute in the strict sense because the impulsive
  limit has no α-structure at all and the finite-$\delta t$
  corrections enter through $[\omega_m a^\dagger a, H_\text{coupling}]$,
  which is a Debye–Waller-class object.

**Not resolved by these lemmas.**
- Closed-form for the $V(|\alpha|)$ oscillation at $\delta t/T_m = 0.80$
  requires the next order in $\omega_m \delta t$; see §4 of the notes.
  This is the remaining WP-C v0.2 analytic task.
- The *shape* (period, phase of the local maximum) of the α-recovery
  curve is still only numerical. An analytic prediction at leading
  non-trivial order in $\omega_m \delta t$ would be the natural v0.2
  closure.
