# Logbook — 2026-04-13 — v0.9.1 pulse-centering closes Fig 6

**Context.** User raised the timing-convention question: "first pulse
should be timed at exactly maximum position for phase zero". Under
v0.9.0, pulse 1 *starts* at the motional position extremum when
φ_α = 0 but then rotates into negative phase territory during δt
(ω_m·δt ≈ 47° at Hasse-matched parameters). Option (B), agreed:
center pulse 1 on the extremum — symmetric about φ_α = 0.

Engine v0.9.1 added `center_pulses_at_phase` (opt-in flag, default
False preserves v0.9.0 behaviour). This entry documents the impact on
WP-V Fig 6 reproduction.

## 1. Headline — the ϑ₀=0 ϕ-asymmetry was a timing artefact

| Quantity                                 | v0.9.0 v3 (Hasse-matched, uncentered) | **v0.9.1 v4 (+centered)** | Hasse |
|------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|-----------------------------|-------|
| σ_z map range, Fig 6a                    | ±0.950                                | ±0.950                      | ±0.9  |
| δ⟨n⟩ map range, Fig 6b                   | ±1.05                                 | ±1.05                       | ±0.9  |
| δ⟨n⟩ ϕ-amplitude **at ϑ₀=0**             | 0.43                                  | **0.005**                   | ≈ 0 (by symmetry) |
| δ⟨n⟩ ϑ₀-amplitude at ϕ=0                 | 1.08                                  | 1.08                        | ≈ 1  (by symmetry) |
| ϕ:ϑ₀ amplitude ratio at the two axes     | 0.40                                  | **0.004**                   | symmetry-sensitive |

The ϕ-amplitude at ϑ₀ = 0 dropped by two orders of magnitude (0.43 →
0.005 ≈ 0) under the physically correct pulse-centering convention.

**Why this is the right answer.** At ϑ₀ = 0, the prepared wave packet
sits at ⟨X⟩ = +|α|·2, ⟨P⟩ = 0 and is symmetric under ϑ₀ → −ϑ₀. The AC
kick direction rotates with ϕ_AC in phase space; the back-action on
⟨n⟩ is therefore the magnitude of the kick's projection onto the wave
packet's quadrature distribution, which must be ϕ-symmetric at a
symmetry point of ϑ. Centered pulses respect this symmetry; v0.9.0
start-of-pulse timing breaks it by biasing the kick toward negative ϑ,
producing a spurious ϕ-dependence of order (ω_m·δt) · (per-pulse θ).

The numerical magnitude matches: the v3 spurious 0.43 amplitude is
order ω_m·δt · (η·|α|) ≈ 0.82 · 0.397 · 3 ≈ 0.97 times a small
prefactor, consistent with a ~0.5 magnitude in dimensionless units.

## 2. Structural agreement with Hasse Fig 6

Both v4 plots now show clear **diagonal stripe patterns**:

- Fig 6a v4: σ_z red/blue bands along (ϑ₀ + ϕ_AC) and (ϑ₀ − ϕ_AC)
  diagonals — the V-shape Hasse Fig 6a shows.
- Fig 6b v4: δ⟨n⟩ four-quadrant purple/green alternation along
  diagonals — the sign-alternating Hasse Fig 6b pattern.

Off-symmetry rows (e.g. ϑ₀ ≈ 3π/4) show strong ⟨n⟩ variation with ϕ
(range spanning [7.96, 10.11]) — genuine Doppler-like signature, the
same physics that produced the 5 % |C| spread in the WP-E S2 revisit.
Symmetry rows (ϑ₀ = 0 and ϑ₀ = π) show minimal ϕ-variation, as
symmetry requires.
