**Two distinct calibration optima at α=0:**
- max σ_z   at Ω ≈ 0.084: σ_z = +0.928 — best alignment with +ẑ.
- min |C|   at Ω ≈ 0.090: |C| = 0.027 — spin lies cleanly on the z-axis.

Neither reaches σ_z = +1, **even at the optimum**. The 7 % shortfall is
real spin-motion entanglement at vacuum: each AC pulse acts via
C = exp(iηX) on |0⟩, which produces a superposition that includes
higher Fock states. Across 30 pulses the cumulative motional
excitation entangles spin with motion, capping the spin Bloch length
below 1.

This is **the same physics that drives Hasse's Fig 8b contrast loss
at α > 0**, just acting at α = 0 too. Hasse calibrates experimentally
to maximise the operation; the engine does it by `max σ_z` or
`min |C|`, both giving the same result up to a small shift.

Sub-issue A is therefore not a bug — it is real spin-motion
entanglement that Hasse also experiences but can hide via experimental
phase tuning. The engine reports it honestly.

## 2. Sub-issue B: revivals persist at every Ω

The right column of the table above shows **2–4 sign-flips in d|C|/dα
at every Ω value**. There is no Ω at the Hasse-matched (N, δt) point
where C(α) is monotone-decreasing. The smallest C(α=8) achievable is
0.48 at Ω ≈ 0.13 — far from Hasse's "≈ 0".

The (Ω, |α|) heatmap and the |C|(α) curve family
([omega_alpha_curves.png](../plots/omega_alpha_curves.png)) make this
visible: every horizontal cut at fixed Ω is a wavy curve with a
characteristic period in α of ~3–4. The wave amplitude shrinks at
larger Ω but does not flatten into a monotone decay.

**Conclusion.** Pure Ω calibration at Hasse-matched timing **cannot**
recover Hasse Fig 8b's smooth shape. The revival structure is intrinsic
to the engine's full unitary spin-motion evolution at this regime.

## 3. Methodological discrepancy hypothesis

Hasse Fig 8 caption: *"Numerical calibrations to convert analysis phase
shifts ϕ₀ and contrast C into (a) position and (b) momentum
[…] for a single ²⁵Mg⁺ ion with initial thermal n_th = 0.15."*

So Fig 8b is a *simulation*, not lab data. If our engine's full
unitary evolution at matched (η, ω_m, Ω, N, δt) gives revivals while
Hasse's simulation gives smooth decay, **the two simulations make
different approximations**.

Plausible methodological differences:

1. **Time-averaged coupling per pulse (analytical).** Hasse Appendix D
   says he implements "a time-dependent Rabi rate Ω = Ω(t) matched to
   our experimental stroboscopic parameters". This may mean he applies
   each pulse as an effective rotation with `Ω_eff(α, η, δt)`
   computed analytically from the time-averaged variance ⟨Var(X_i)⟩_δt
   (his Fig 3a inset language). That gives Bessel-like monotone
   suppression with α — exactly Hasse Fig 8b shape.

   Our engine instead applies the full Hamiltonian
   H = ω_m a†a + (Ω/2)(C σ₋ + C† σ₊) for δt with `expm`, which is
   the exact unitary for the joint spin-motion system. Exact unitary
   includes coherent revivals; analytical time-averaging washes them
   out by construction.

2. **Ensemble averaging over a hidden parameter.** Hasse Fig 4 and
   Fig 8 both involve calibrations from "numerical predictions for our
   chosen parameters". If those predictions average over a phase
   ensemble (e.g., φ_α uniform [0, 2π] before extracting C), the
   revival nulls average out. Worth checking against our v3 run, which
   used fixed φ_α = 0.

3. **Different parameter regime than Table II suggests.** Hasse's
   Table II lists Ω/(2π) = 0.3 MHz as the AC laser power. The
   *effective per-pulse Rabi* used in his Fig 8 simulation may be a
   lab-tuned value much smaller than this — i.e., the experimental
   "for-loop optimisation" picks a per-pulse θ that puts him into an
   anharmonic regime our naive matching missed.

The discrepancy is **diagnostic** of Hasse's simulation methodology.
This is the kind of finding a code consistency check is supposed to
expose.

## 4. Round 3 verdict

WP-V's job is to use Hasse's published numerical panels as a
consistency check on our engine. Round 3 outcome:

- **Engine v0.9 reproduces Hasse Fig 6** (a + b) to within 5–17 % on
  amplitudes, with the load-bearing ϕ-dependence on δ⟨n⟩ correctly
  generated. ✅
- **Engine v0.9 does not reproduce Hasse Fig 8b** at any (Ω, N, δt)
  combination consistent with Hasse's stated parameters. ❌
- The Fig 8b discrepancy is **not a bug**; it points at a difference
  in *simulation methodology* between our exact-unitary approach and
  whatever per-pulse averaging Hasse uses. That difference can be
  characterised but resolving it requires reading Hasse's QuTip code,
  not adjusting ours.

This is a clean closing position for WP-V. The engine is now a
*systematic* implementation of Hasse Eq. C1 + Appendix D protocol with
all known approximations made explicit (D1–D6 toggles); Hasse Fig 8b
appears to use a more aggressive per-pulse approximation that we have
not yet identified. Documented as a known gap.
