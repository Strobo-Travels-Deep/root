## 2. Fig 8 v3 — contrast still oscillatory, with new sub-issues

Run table at tilt 0°, n_th = 0.15:

| `|α|` | σ_z   | C  / C(α=0) |
|------|-------|-------------|
| 0    | +0.88 | 1.00 (ref)  |
| 1    | +0.70 | 5.6         |
| 2    | +0.13 | 8.5         |
| 3    | −0.50 | 7.3         |
| 4    | −0.90 | 2.7         |
| 5    | −0.87 | 3.5         |
| 6    | −0.43 | 7.9         |
| 7    | +0.21 | 8.6         |
| 8    | +0.72 | 5.6         |

Oscillation period in α is roughly 4 — periodic structure persists at
the Hasse-matched timing. The expected smooth Hasse Fig 8b decay does
**not** emerge.

Two new sub-issues exposed by Round 2:

### Sub-issue A — α=0 train is not a clean π/2

σ_z(α=0) = +0.88, not +1.00. After MW π/2 (equator) + AC π/2 train
should give σ_z = ±1. The 12 % shortfall means our derived Ω
**under-rotates** the spin: the budget calculation N·Ω_eff·δt = π/2
neglects intra-pulse averaging.

When intra_pulse_motion=True, the "effective π/2 budget" is reduced by
an intra-pulse Bessel-like factor that depends on (η, ω_m·δt). This is
exactly the smearing factor that suppresses Hasse's contrast at high α
— but it also suppresses the carrier rotation at α = 0.

Fix sketch: implement a calibration loop in the driver. Scan Ω until
σ_z(α=0, ϕ_AC=0, ϑ₀=0) reaches the target (e.g. cos⁻¹(0) = 0). This
mirrors Hasse's experimental "for loop optimisation" of the π/2 pulse
train.

### Sub-issue B — strong α-dependent over/under rotation

σ_z swings −0.94 → +0.72 over α ∈ [0, 8] is consistent with the train
rotating by *more than* π/2 at α ≈ 4, then less, then more again. This
is the "α-dependent effective coupling" that Hasse shows as monotonic
*decay*, but in our engine appears as a **revival**, not a decay.

Possible root causes:
1. The engine's chosen Ω is too small relative to ω_m, putting per-pulse
   evolution in a regime where motion dominates over coupling. Hasse's
   actual experimental Ω-during-pulse may be different from his Table II
   value (his table gives the maximum laser power; the per-pulse used
   value may be tuned much lower with different timing).
2. Hasse's per-pulse phase compensation ("incorporating calculated
   delays" in Appendix D) actively breaks the revival structure that
   pure unitary evolution would produce — i.e. the smooth decay in
   Fig 8b is partly an artefact of Hasse's phase calibration.
3. A genuine engine bug in how Ufree handles the gap when D1 is on.

To discriminate: cheap follow-up — sweep Ω at fixed (N=30, δt=0.628)
and look for a Ω value where σ_z(α=0) hits exactly 0 (clean π/2) AND
contrast at α=8 falls below 0.3. If such a point exists, Hasse Fig 8b
is recoverable purely by Ω-tuning. If it doesn't, root cause #2 or #3.

Plots: [fig8a_position_v3.png](../plots/fig8a_position_v3.png),
[fig8b_momentum_v3.png](../plots/fig8b_momentum_v3.png).

## 3. Round 2 verdict

**Fig 6:** amplitudes essentially fixed (within 5–17 % of Hasse).
Residual ϕ-asymmetry is a phase-tuning question, not a physics bug.
Acceptable as a code consistency check; flagged for Round 3 polish.

**Fig 8:** structurally still mismatched. The engine produces revivals
at every (N, δt) point I've tested, including the Hasse-matched one. The
diagnosis above splits Fig 8b's mismatch into a **calibration question**
(Sub-issue A: tune Ω experimentally) and a **physics/protocol question**
(Sub-issue B: are revivals a real prediction at uncalibrated Ω?).

The Round 2 take-away: the engine's *forward dynamics* now matches
Hasse's regime quantitatively (ω_m·δt, η, N all matched). What separates
us from a perfect Fig 8b reproduction is the Hasse-side **experimental
calibration loop**, not engine physics.

## 4. Round 3 plan (proposed, not run)

1. **Calibration loop in the driver.** Add a function
   `calibrate_omega_for_pi2(params)` that bisects on Ω until
   σ_z(α=0) hits the π/2 target. Use the calibrated Ω in Fig 8 v4.
2. **Ω scan diagnostic.** Sweep Ω ∈ [0.05, 0.30] at fixed (N=30, δt,
   α ∈ {0, 4, 8}) and plot the (Ω, |α|) → σ_z surface. If a
   regime exists where σ_z(α=0) is calibrated AND C(α=8) ≪ C(α=0),
   that's the operating point.
3. **Per-pulse phase compensation (D2 extension).** Optionally extend
   ac_phase_deg to a per-pulse list `ac_phase_schedule_deg`. If
   Hasse's calibration is per-pulse phase progression, this is the
   knob that produces it.
