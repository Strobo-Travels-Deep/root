# Extraction — Mirkhalaf & Mølmer (2012)

**Citation key:** [STO12]
**Full reference:** S. S. Mirkhalaf and K. Mølmer,
*Sympathetic Wigner-function tomography of a dark trapped ion*,
Phys. Rev. A **85**, 042109 (2012). Published 12 April 2012.
DOI: [10.1103/PhysRevA.85.042109](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevA.85.042109).
ArXiv: [1110.4804](https://arxiv.org/abs/1110.4804).

**Authors verified:** Safoura Sadat Mirkhalaf and Klaus Mølmer. **NOT
"Casanova et al."** — that conflation with FH20's ref [33] (Casanova
et al., *Eur. Phys. J. D* **66**, 222, 2012) is a citation-mixup; both
2012 papers are different.

**Extraction date:** 2026-05-15.

**One-line summary.** Proposes **sympathetic Wigner-function
tomography**: reconstruct the motional Wigner function of a *dark*
trapped ion (lacking suitable optical transitions for direct state
measurement) by mapping its motional state onto a shared collective
vibrational mode with a co-trapped *bright* ion, then reading out via
**sideband excitation** of the bright ion.

**Why it matters for WP-W.** [STO12] is a **contextual reference**,
not a foundational one. It demonstrates that trapped-ion Wigner-
function tomography is sufficiently developed by 2012 to support
extensions to mixed-species traps and dark ions. For WP-W, [STO12]
is relevant only as background for the broader trapped-ion phase-
space tomography programme — it does *not* anchor any specific WP-W
claim.

-----

## 1. Core proposal

Two-ion trap with one *bright* ion (with cycling transition for
fluorescence readout) and one *dark* ion (without).
The motional modes of the pair are *collective* — both centre-of-mass
and stretch modes involve both ions.

Protocol:
1. Map the dark ion's motional state onto a chosen collective
   vibrational eigenmode through controlled exchange operations.
2. Apply sideband excitations to the bright ion targeting that
   collective mode.
3. Read out fluorescence on the bright ion to extract the collective
   mode's Fock populations.
4. Reconstruct the original motional state of the dark ion from those
   populations.

The exact tomography variant (displaced-Fock-population sum vs.
displaced parity vs. direct $\chi$) is not specified at the abstract
level; the methodology emphasises sideband excitation as the readout
mechanism on the bright ion.

## 2. Relation to WP-W

[STO12] is **off the WP-W main lineage**:

- WP-W targets single-species Mg⁺ at $\eta = 0.40$ — no second species, no sympathetic readout.
- The sympathetic-tomography extension is orthogonal to WP-W's
  stroboscopic-comb adaptation question.
- [STO12]'s protocol applies in principle on top of either the
  displaced-Fock or direct-$\chi$ readout chain — it's a
  *measurement-target abstraction layer*, not a competing protocol.

For WP-W, [STO12] should be cited as **broader context for
trapped-ion Wigner tomography**, not as part of the core lineage
([CG69] → [Wal95] → [LMKMIW96] / [LD97] → [Bertet02] / [FH20]).

## 4. Contextual paragraph (for `contextual-notes.md`)

[STO12] (Mirkhalaf & Mølmer, *PRA* 85, 042109, 2012) extends
trapped-ion Wigner tomography to **mixed-species traps**: a dark ion's
motional state can be characterised by mapping onto a shared
collective mode and reading out via fluorescence on a co-trapped
bright ion. This is **off WP-W's main lineage** — WP-W is single-
species — but represents the broader experimental sophistication of
the trapped-ion phase-space tomography programme by 2012. WP-W cites
[STO12] only as contextual background.
