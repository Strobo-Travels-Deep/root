## 1. Platform

- Single **вЃґвЃ°CaвЃє ion** (NOT MgвЃє; different platform from Hasse 2024 / WP-W).
- Axial motional mode $\omega_m/(2\pi) \approx 1.9$ MHz.
- Optical qubit: $|{\downarrow}\rangle = |{}^2S_{1/2}, m_j = 1/2\rangle$
  and $|{\uparrow}\rangle = |{}^2D_{5/2}, m_j = 3/2\rangle$.
- **LambвЂ“Dicke parameter $\eta \approx 0.05$** вЂ” *eight times smaller*

- SDF carrier strength $\Omega/(2\pi) \approx 20$ kHz.

## 2. Protocol вЂ” the "characteristic function readout circuit" (Fig 1)

Quantum circuit, applied to $|{\uparrow}\rangle \otimes |\psi_\text{in}\rangle$:

1. **Carrier rotation** $\hat R(\theta, \varphi = 0) = \cos(\theta/2)\mathbb 1 - i\sin(\theta/2)\hat Y$
   (rotation of the spin around $-\hat Y$ by angle $\theta$, $\hat X$ and $\hat Y$ being Pauli operators).
2. **State-dependent displacement** $\hat D(\alpha(t)\hat X)$ via a
   **bichromatic laser pulse** (simultaneous red + blue sideband drive)
   with $\alpha(t) = \eta\Omega t\,e^{-i\Delta\varphi/2}$. The bichromatic
   $\Omega, \Delta\varphi$ control the displacement amplitude and phase.
3. **Fluorescence readout** of $|{\downarrow}\rangle$ population.

The output observable (eq. 3 of FH20):

$$
\langle\hat Z\rangle = \tfrac{1}{2}\langle e^{i\theta}\hat D(-\beta) + e^{-i\theta}\hat D(\beta)\rangle = \cos\theta\,\mathrm{Re}[\chi(\beta)] + \sin\theta\,\mathrm{Im}[\chi(\beta)].
$$

So $\theta = 0$ gives Re $\chi$; $\theta = \pi/2$ gives Im $\chi$. **Two readout settings per $\beta$ point** yield the complex $\chi(\beta)$.

### 2.1 Crucial design choice: bichromatic, not stroboscopic

The SDF in FH20 is a **CW bichromatic laser pulse** вЂ” simultaneous red
and blue sideband drive вЂ” not a stroboscopic train. In the LD-resolved-
sideband limit, the bichromatic Hamiltonian reduces to
$H_\text{SDF} = \eta\Omega\hat X(a\,e^{-i\Delta\varphi/2} + a^\dagger e^{i\Delta\varphi/2})/2$,
i.e. a clean spin-$\hat X$-conditioned displacement. After exponentiation
this gives exactly $\hat D(\alpha\hat X)$.

**This is the "ideal SDF" of WP-W's В§Analytical**, realized natively by
the FH20 protocol. The Пѓ_x coupling is equivalent to Пѓ_z up to a fixed
basis rotation; what matters is that the *spin observable* controls the
*motional displacement* cleanly, without the carrier-rotation
contamination that Hasse's monochromatic AC train carries (WP-W В§7#3).

So FH20's protocol *does* what WP-W's В§7#3 "no limit recovers ideal
SDF" says the Hasse engine cannot. The difference is the SDF
implementation (bichromatic vs. monochromatic-stroboscopic), not the
inversion chain.

## 3. Reconstruction pipeline вЂ” full FFT, not cuts

For each $\beta$ on a **uniformly-spaced square grid** (i.e. Cartesian,
not polar вЂ” matches WP-W В§2's Dirichlet-Cartesian rule by construction):

1. Two measurements ($\theta = 0$ and $\theta = \pi/2$) в†’ complex $\chi(\beta)$.
2. Subtract a SPAM bias parameter $b$ (fit from the model
   $E(\beta) = \chi(\beta)(1 - |b|) + b$).
3. Zero-pad outside the measurement range.
4. (Optional) resample to equidistant grid.
5. **2D DFT** of $\chi$ в†’ $W(\gamma)$ via eq. 2 of FH20:
   $$
   W_l(\gamma) = \frac{1}{\pi^2}\int \chi(\beta)\,e^{l|\beta|^2/2}\,e^{\gamma\beta^* - \gamma^*\beta}\,d^2\beta
   $$
   with $l = 0$ for Wigner ($l = -1$ Husimi-Q, $l = +1$ Glauber-Sudarshan).

**This is byte-for-byte the WP-W D3 reconstruction pipeline** (В§4 D3,
reconstruction-demo script). FH20 already implements it; WP-W's
implementation is a re-implementation on a different platform.

### 4.1 Parity from the Fourier transform (eq. 7)

For the squeezed cat, FH20 extracts the **parity** $\langle\hat P\rangle = \pi W(0)/2$
directly from the integral of Re$\,\chi$:

$$
\langle\hat P\rangle = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int \mathrm{Re}[\chi(\beta)]\,d^2\beta.
$$

Measured $\langle\hat P\rangle = 0.98$ (with SPAM-corrected bias) вЂ” confirms
the cat's quantum coherence.

This is a **non-FFT diagnostic** that WP-W could adopt for its v0.5
back-action study: parity from the П‡-integral is grid-independent.

### 4.2 GKP-code state reconstruction (Fig 4)

FH20 reconstructs an approximate three-component GKP code state at
$l_c = 2.50$, $r_c = 0.93$. Measures only the positive quadrant of
Re$\,\chi$ (using the state's known symmetry to mirror), then
2D-DFTs. This is **the most non-Gaussian state ever reconstructed
via direct $\chi$-tomography in trapped ions** at time of writing.

For WP-W: the v0.4 deferral of GKP to a separate WP (В§7#4) is
*conservative* relative to FH20's demonstrated capability. The
deferral is about grid resolution вЂ” FH20's $\eta = 0.05$ permits
much finer $\beta$ sampling. **WP-W's GKP barrier is grid-resolution,
not principle.**

## 5. Performance / efficiency

- **One readout per $\beta$ point per real/imag component** (vs. ~20+ for prior Fock-state-based Wigner reconstructions).
- Measurement time per phase-space point: roughly factor of 20 improvement over prior methods.
- Square-fidelity between calibrated and fitted pure states: > 0.98 for all four states.
- Numerical DFT error from windowing / zero-padding: < 0.5% typically.
