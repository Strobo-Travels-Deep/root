## 1. Apparatus and conventions

### 1.1 Ion and qubit

- Single **²⁵Mg⁺** ion, nuclear spin 5/2.
- Qubit: stretched ground-state hyperfine pair
  $|{\downarrow}\rangle = |F=3,\,m_F=3\rangle$ and
  $|{\uparrow}\rangle = |F=2,\,m_F=2\rangle$.
- Transition frequency $\omega_S/(2\pi) \simeq 1.8$ GHz.
- Magnetic field $|B| \simeq 0.58$ mT, RF trap $\omega_\text{RF}/(2\pi) \simeq 56.3$ MHz.

### 1.2 Three motional modes

| mode | frequency $\omega_m/(2\pi)$ | direction | Lamb–Dicke $\eta$ |
|---|---|---|---|
| LF | 1.3 MHz | axial (along $z$) | $\eta_\text{LF} = 0.40$ (AC); 0 (MW, CC) |
| MF | 2.9 MHz | $x$–$y$ plane, ~30° from $y$ | $\eta_\text{MF} = 0.23$ (AC) |
| HF | 4.5 MHz | $x$–$y$ plane | $\eta_\text{HF} = 0.18$ (AC) |

**WP-W operates on the LF mode** ($\eta = 0.40$, $\omega_m/(2\pi) = 1.3$ MHz).
Engine settings match: `eta=0.397`, `omega_m=1.3`.

### 1.3 Drive fields

Three independent control fields, all phase-locked to a 10-MHz GPS-referenced master clock:

- **MW** (gigahertz): global microwave field, $\omega_\text{MW}/(2\pi) \simeq \omega_S$. Carrier-resonant, motion-insensitive ($\eta_\text{MW} = 0$).
- **CC** (collinear configuration): TPSR beams overlapped, motion-insensitive in the trap region ($\eta = 0$, $\lambda_\text{eff} \geq 80$ mm).
- **AC** (antiparallel configuration): TPSR beams counterpropagating, generates polarization-gradient traveling-wave (PGTW) pattern. $\lambda_\text{eff} = 140$ nm, $\eta_\text{LF} = 0.40$, drives motional couplings.

Rabi rates: $\Omega_\text{MW}/(2\pi) = 0.1$ MHz, $\Omega_\text{CC}/(2\pi) = 0.5$ MHz, $\Omega_\text{AC}/(2\pi) = 0.3$ MHz.

The AC drive is the **WP-W's "analysis pulse train"** — directly relevant.

### 1.4 Coherent-displacement preparation

$D(\alpha)$ pulse generated by a single dedicated electrode at $\omega_\text{LF}$, with tunable amplitude $|\alpha|$ and phase $\vartheta_0$:

$$
\alpha = |\alpha|\,\exp(i\vartheta_0).
$$

The phase $\vartheta_0$ is **locked to the MW** (and hence to the analysis-train phase reference). This is the motional-phase convention WP-W inherits.

## 2. The Hamiltonian (Appendix C, eq. C1)

The full spin–motion interaction Hamiltonian during a TPSR pulse:

$$
\boxed{\;H_\text{TI} = \frac{\hbar\omega_z}{2}\sigma_z + \hbar\omega_m a^\dagger a + \frac{\hbar\Omega}{2}\bigl[C(\eta, a, a^\dagger)\,\sigma_- + C(\eta, a, a^\dagger)^\dagger\,\sigma_+\bigr]\;}
$$

with the coupling operator

$$
C(\eta, a, a^\dagger) = \exp\!\bigl[i\eta\,(a^\dagger + a)\bigr]
$$

and Pauli operators acting on the qubit subspace.

**This is identical to the engine implementation** in
[`scripts/stroboscopic/hamiltonian.py`](../../../scripts/stroboscopic/hamiltonian.py):
- `ham.build_pulse_hamiltonian(eta, omega_r, delta, nmax, C, Cdag, ac_phase_rad, omega_m, intra_pulse_motion)`
  produces $H_\text{eng} = (\delta/2)\sigma_z + \omega_m a^\dagger a + (\Omega_r/2)[e^{i\varphi} C \sigma_- + e^{-i\varphi} C^\dagger \sigma_+]$,
- which is $H_\text{TI}$ in a frame rotating at the spin transition frequency, with $\omega_z$ replaced by detuning $\delta$ and the analysis-train AC phase $\varphi$ added.

**Notation cross-check with WP-W:**

| Hasse 2024 | WP-W |
|---|---|
| $\Omega$ (Rabi) | $\Omega_r$ |
| $\eta$ (LD) | $\eta$ |
| $C(\eta, a, a^\dagger)$ | $C$ |
| $\vartheta_0$ (motional displacement phase) | $\theta_\alpha$ |
| $\varphi$ (AC analysis phase) | $\varphi_\text{train}$ |
| $\delta_0$ (analysis detuning, implicit) | $\delta$ |

**Note:** Hasse uses $\vartheta$ for the *motional* state phase (the phase of the coherent state $\alpha$) and $\varphi$ for the *analysis* phase (the phase of the AC pulse train). WP-W uses $\theta_\alpha$ and $\varphi_\text{train}$ respectively. Same physics, different names.

## 3. Method — the stroboscopic measurement sequence

### 3.1 Sequence (Fig 3a in Hasse 2024)

1. Initialize ion in $|{\downarrow}\rangle$, Doppler cool, 3D sideband cool to $\langle n\rangle_\text{th} < 0.2$.
2. Static shim displacement to set $(\Delta x_0, \Delta y_0, \Delta z_0)$ if needed (precalibrated).
3. **$D(\alpha)$ pulse**: coherent excitation via single electrode, 5 μs duration, $\alpha = |\alpha|\,e^{i\vartheta_0}$.
4. **MW $\pi/2$ synchronization pulse**: takes spin from $|{\downarrow}\rangle$ to the equator, phase-locks spin to the MW reference. Duration ~5 μs.
5. **AC stroboscopic train**: $N_S = 30$ flashes of duration $\delta t \simeq 100$ ns, cycle period $\Delta t = 2\pi/\omega_\text{LF} \simeq 770$ ns, total train length ~23 μs. Variable analysis phase $\varphi$ between flashes.
6. **Detect $|{\downarrow}\rangle$ population $P_{\downarrow}$** via fluorescence (PMT, ~30 μs).

This is **WP-W's protocol exactly**, with WP-W's $N_\text{rec} = 80$ taking the place of Hasse's $N_S = 30$.

### 3.2 The 2D scan

For fixed $|\alpha|$, Hasse scans both $\varphi$ (analysis phase, 0 to $2\pi$) and $\vartheta_0$ (motional displacement phase, 0 to $2\pi$). The result is a 2D heatmap in $(\varphi, \vartheta_0)$ space.

**This is the same 2D map that WP-W proposes to FFT.** Hasse's Fig 6a (numerical) and Fig 7 (experimental) show this map directly for $|\alpha| = 3$ (numerical) and $|\alpha| = 3.4(1)$ (experimental), spanning $\langle\sigma_z\rangle \in [-0.9, +0.9]$.

### 3.3 Position and momentum readout (Fig 4)

Two distinct readout signals from the 2D map:

- **Position $\langle X\rangle$**: encoded *linearly* in the analysis phase shift $\varphi_0$.
- **Momentum magnitude $|\langle P\rangle|$**: encoded *nonlinearly* in the contrast variation $\Delta C$, "due to the finite-time effects of the flashes."

This is critical: Hasse measures $X$ and $|P|$ — *not* the full Wigner function. They use cuts along $\vartheta_0$ and $\varphi$ separately.

**WP-W's contribution here**: the same 2D map can be reinterpreted as a sampling of $\chi(\beta)$ on a polar grid, then FFT'd to $W(\alpha)$ — i.e. *full* phase-space reconstruction, not just $\langle X\rangle$ and $|\langle P\rangle|$.

## 4. Key results

### 4.1 Sensitivity / noise floors

- **Position**: noise floor $1.8(2)$ nm.
- **Momentum**: noise floor $8(2)$ zN μs.
- Temporal resolution $\lesssim 100$ ns.

### 4.2 Superresolution

The 140-nm PGTW pattern is resolved with effective probe size ~10 nm (ground-state wavefunction width). This is "superresolution" in the sense that the ion's wavepacket is much smaller than $\lambda_\text{eff}/2$.

### 4.3 Phase stability (Appendix B, Table I)

Short-term ($t = 2$ s): MW–AC phase variance $11.8(5)°$. Long-term ($t = 200$ s): $37(3)°$. Coherence duration ~70 μs. Pattern stability $\lesssim 0.1$ rad after interleaved recalibration.

### 4.4 Back-action (Appendix D, Fig 6b)

**Hasse defines back-action as $\delta\langle n\rangle = \langle n\rangle_\text{fin} - \langle n\rangle_\text{ini}$** — change in mean phonon number through the measurement.

For coherent input $|\alpha = 3\rangle$ ($\langle n\rangle_\text{coh} = 9$), Hasse shows a 2D map of $\delta\langle n\rangle(\varphi, \vartheta_0)$ with structured negative (purple) and positive (green) regions spanning $\delta\langle n\rangle \in [-0.9, +0.9]$.

Quote: *"modulated interactions can be fine-tuned either to enable minimally invasive measurements of phase-space properties or to allow for coherent, spin-phase-dependent steering of phonon states."*

**Implication for WP-W's v0.5 back-action scope:** Hasse already *demonstrates* the back-action map qualitatively. WP-W's contribution would be the *quantitative* comparison between the ideal-SDF prediction (50/50 mixture of displaced copies, see §Analytical of WP-W) and the native-engine map — *not* "first characterisation of back-action."

### 4.5 Squeezed states (Appendix E, Fig 9)

Hasse extends the framework to squeezed states:
- Squeezing operator $S(\zeta) = \exp[\tfrac{1}{2}(\zeta^* a^2 - \zeta a^{\dagger 2})]$.
- Squeezed vacuum $|\zeta\rangle = S(\zeta)|0\rangle$.
- **Critical timing change**: $\Delta t = 2\pi/(2\omega_\text{LF})$ — half the displaced-state cycle period. The stroboscopic train syncs to the *quadrature* dynamics, which evolve at $2\omega_\text{LF}$.
- Numerical 2D map of $\langle\sigma_z\rangle(\varphi, \zeta_0)$ and back-action $\delta\langle n\rangle(\varphi, \zeta_0)$ for $\langle n\rangle_\text{sq} = 1$.
- Squeezed positions $\zeta_0 = \pi$ yield *higher* contrast than vacuum; squeezed momenta $\zeta_0 = 0$ yield *lower* contrast.

**Implication for WP-W's v0.5 squeezed-vacuum scope:** Hasse already provides the framework. WP-W's contribution would be the χ-function tomography reframing + the Wigner FFT inversion *for squeezed states*, building on Hasse's $\Delta t = 2\pi/(2\omega_\text{LF})$ timing.

## 5. Hasse's interpretation vs. WP-W's reframing

**Hasse's framing:**
- "Stroboscopic tracking of dynamic changes in phase space."
- The protocol *encodes* position and momentum in the spin observable.
- Decoding via specific calibration functions from numerical simulations (Appendix D, Fig 8).
- No mention of: characteristic function $\chi$, Wigner function $W$, Fourier inversion, Lutterbach–Davidovich, Cahill–Glauber, Flühmann–Home.

**WP-W's reframing:**
- The 2D scan is a sampling of $\chi_{\rho_m}(\beta)$ on a polar grid.
- $\beta = \beta(\delta, \varphi_\text{train}; N)$ via the inverse Dirichlet rule.
- 2D FFT of $\chi$ on a Cartesian-targeted grid → $W(\alpha)$.
- Anchored in [CG69] (formalism), [LD97] (displaced-parity origin), [FH20] (trapped-ion implementation).

**The substantive WP-W contributions** are therefore:

1. **Theoretical reinterpretation.** Identifies the measurement as direct characteristic-function tomography in the Lutterbach–Davidovich / Flühmann–Home lineage.
2. **Full Wigner reconstruction** (not just $\langle X\rangle$ and $|\langle P\rangle|$ cuts).
3. **Non-Gaussian benchmark set.** Hasse demonstrates coherent + squeezed; WP-W adds Fock $|1\rangle$, $|2\rangle$, pure cat, mixed cat.
4. **Ideal-SDF vs. native-Raman separation.** Hasse uses the native Raman pulse throughout; WP-W explicitly separates the ideal $D(\sigma_z\beta)$ chain from the native $C\sigma_- + \text{h.c.}$ implementation (WP-W §7#3).
5. **The Dirichlet-Cartesian grid targeting** (WP-W §2). Hasse uses a polar $(\varphi, \vartheta_0)$ scan; WP-W's inverse-Dirichlet rule lands the scan directly on FFT-friendly Cartesian $\beta$ nodes.

**The substantive WP-W contributions that v0.4 does *not* yet claim novelty for:**

- The protocol itself (Hasse's prior art).
- The back-action *qualitative* observation (Hasse Fig 6b).
- The squeezed-state extension *concept* (Hasse Appendix E).

WP-W's v0.4 introduction (§1 of `WORK-PROGRAM.md`) currently understates the Hasse 2024 antecedent — it credits Hasse for the engine and the experimental platform, but it does not make explicit that Hasse 2024 *is* the protocol WP-W reinterprets. **The lit-review pass should fix this in v0.4 polish.**

## 8. Contextual note (to seed `contextual-notes.md`)

Hasse 2024 is the **direct experimental antecedent** of WP-W. The Hasse / Warring / Schaetz group at Freiburg developed the stroboscopic AC-Raman pulse-train protocol, established its phase stability, demonstrated position and momentum readout for coherent displaced states, and outlined back-action and squeezed-state extensions — all *experimentally*, anchored in their trapped-ion sensing programme.

WP-W reinterprets the same protocol through the **characteristic-function tomography** lens of [LD97] (cavity-QED origins) and [FH20] (modern trapped-ion implementation). The key WP-W moves are: (i) recognise the 2D $(\varphi, \vartheta_0)$ scan as a polar sampling of $\chi(\beta)$; (ii) Dirichlet-Cartesian targeting for FFT inversion; (iii) full $W(\alpha)$ reconstruction rather than $\langle X\rangle$ / $|\langle P\rangle|$ cuts; (iv) non-Gaussian state benchmarks (Fock, cat); (v) explicit ideal-SDF / native-Raman separation.

The relationship is **complementary, not redundant**: Hasse establishes the experiment; WP-W contributes the theoretical reframing and full-state reconstruction pipeline.
