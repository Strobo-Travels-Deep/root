## 1. Core protocol — displaced parity via dispersive coupling

The Wigner function of a bosonic mode in state $\rho$ is

$$
W(\alpha) = \frac{2}{\pi}\,\mathrm{Tr}\!\bigl[\hat D^\dagger(\alpha)\,\rho\,\hat D(\alpha)\,\hat P\bigr] = \frac{2}{\pi}\bigl\langle \hat D^\dagger(\alpha)\,\hat P\,\hat D(\alpha)\bigr\rangle_{\rho}
$$

with $\hat P = (-1)^{\hat n} = e^{i\pi \hat n}$ the photon-number
parity operator, and $\hat D(\alpha)$ the standard displacement
operator. LD97's contribution is to recognise this expression as
**directly measurable** by a single-atom probe protocol — no need for
inverse Radon transform of marginal distributions, no need for
homodyne tomography.

### 1.1 Cavity-QED implementation (LD97 primary)

1. Prepare cavity field in state $\rho$ to be characterised.
2. **Displace** the cavity field by $-\alpha$ (classical injection): $\rho \to \hat D(-\alpha)\rho\,\hat D^\dagger(-\alpha)$.
3. Send a circular Rydberg atom through the cavity in dispersive (off-resonant) regime. The atom acquires a light shift proportional to the cavity photon number.
4. **Ramsey interferometry** on the same atom: a π/2 pulse before and after the cavity, with a phase choice that maps photon parity to atomic state population.
5. Read out the atomic state. The atomic-population difference (between the two Ramsey output ports) is proportional to $\langle\hat P\rangle$ at displaced phase-space point $\alpha$.

The protocol is **insensitive to low detection efficiency** because
the Wigner function is bounded and the readout is a single-shot
population measurement, not a photon count.

### 1.2 Trapped-ion implementation (LD97 secondary)

LD97 explicitly notes the protocol applies to the **vibronic state of a
trapped ion** as well — the atom-cavity dispersive interaction is
replaced by the off-resonant spin-motion coupling in the Lamb–Dicke
regime, and Ramsey interferometry runs on the ion's internal qubit
states. *This trapped-ion variant is the direct ancestor of the
WP-W / Hasse / Flühmann measurement schemes.*

## 2. Lineage placement

**Predecessors:**
- The general phase-space formalism of [CG69] (Cahill & Glauber) — Wigner function as Fourier dual of the characteristic function.
- [Wal95] Wallentowitz & Vogel (*PRL* 75, 2932, 1995) — earliest direct-χ proposal (FH20 ref [30]). [LD97] focuses on direct-W via parity; [Wal95] focuses on direct-χ via different measurement scheme.

**Direct experimental implementations:**
- [Bertet02] Bertet, Auffeves, Maioli, Osnaghi, Meunier, Brune, Raimond, Haroche, *PRL* **89**, 200402 (2002) — first cQED implementation, Rydberg atoms in microwave cavity.
- Deleglise et al., *Nature* **455**, 510 (2008) — quantum-state tomography of decohering cavity fields (extends [Bertet02]).
- [Hof09] Hofheinz et al., *Nature* **459**, 546 (2009) — implementation in superconducting cQED (qubit + microwave resonator).
- [LMKMIW96] Leibfried et al. (1996) — earlier *trapped-ion* implementation using a different (Fock-population-based) approach, not directly LD97.

**Variants and extensions:**
- [Bertet02]'s improvement: simultaneously measure both Re and Im of $\langle\hat D(\alpha)\rangle$ → full $\chi(\alpha)$ rather than just $W$. This becomes the FH20 generalisation.
- LD97's parity-readout becomes generalised to *characteristic-function readout* in [FH20] via the bichromatic SDF + carrier rotation protocol — and that is what WP-W's §Analytical bullet 2 implements *with stroboscopic AC pulses* in place of the bichromatic CW drive.

## 3. What [LD97] does NOT do

To pin down WP-W's contributions vs. [LD97] specifically:

- LD97 is a *theoretical proposal*, not an experimental demonstration.
- LD97 assumes a *dispersive* (off-resonant) interaction — qualitatively
  different from the *resonant* monochromatic AC train of Hasse 2024
  and WP-W. LD97's interaction time enters as a single light shift, not
  as a comb of stroboscopic pulses.
- LD97 does not specify a Fourier-transform inversion pipeline — it
  measures $W(\alpha)$ point-by-point via displaced parity. The
  $\chi \to W$ FFT inversion is [Wal95] / [FH20].
- LD97 does not address the high-η or saturated-rotation regimes.
- LD97 does not address back-action on the bosonic state.

So WP-W inherits *the displaced-readout idea* from LD97 (cited as
lineage), but the **technical chain** WP-W actually uses is closer to
FH20's direct-χ + 2D-FFT structure than to LD97's displaced-parity.

## 5. Contextual paragraph (for `contextual-notes.md`)

[LD97] proposed the **displaced-parity scheme**: measure
$\langle\hat D^\dagger(\alpha)\hat P\hat D(\alpha)\rangle$
on a bosonic mode via dispersive qubit interaction + Ramsey
interferometry, giving the Wigner function point-by-point as
$W(\alpha) = (2/\pi)\langle\ldots\rangle$. The protocol applies to
both cavity-QED photon fields and trapped-ion motional modes, and
its key practical virtue is insensitivity to detection efficiency.

LD97 is the **theoretical origin of all direct-Wigner-measurement
trapped-ion / cQED protocols** that followed: [Bertet02] (first cQED
implementation), [Hof09] (cQED in superconducting circuits), and
[FH20] (trapped-ion direct-$\chi$ tomography that generalises
parity-readout to Re/Im-of-$\langle\hat D\rangle$ readout). WP-W's
spiritual debt to LD97 is the "qubit-observable measures phase-space
function" idea; the technical chain WP-W actually implements is the
FH20 generalisation — direct-$\chi$ + 2D-FFT — adapted to the
stroboscopic monochromatic AC train of [Hasse24].
