WP-W — Characteristic-function tomography under a high-η monochromatic stroboscopic drive

A numerical adaptation of the Flühmann–Home ([FH20], PRL 125 043602) direct characteristic-function → FFT Wigner framework to the monochromatic stroboscopic AC-train drive of [Hasse24] (PRA 109 053105), at high Lamb–Dicke parameter η = 0.40. It is a theory/numerics continuation of the Hasse-2024 ²⁵Mg⁺ programme — not an experimental result, and explicitly subordinate to [FH20] (the χ-FFT framework) and [Hasse24] (the protocol, the back-action and squeezed-state concepts).

What was done

  • Core chain validated (P0/D2/D3/P1/D4); engine bridge: Layer A bit-exact, Layer B engine-χ vs analytic-χ = 3.75×10⁻⁴ over 6481 nodes.
  • Quantitative ideal-vs-native back-action map across the full §7#4 set on the carrier and first sideband, with a tooth-robust quantum/classical discriminator.
  • Squeezed vacuum: the 𝒪(η²)/δt analytic pass (locked); ideal reconstruction clears the §7#5 Gaussian tier; and the native 𝒪(η²) re-audit — the monochromatic engine does not cleanly engineer the 2ω_m two-phonon squeezing channel (engine-specific; [FH20]'s bichromatic drive realises the ideal χ-measurement chain).
