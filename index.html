Headline findings — strong / weak binding

The stroboscopic protocol sits near the boundary between two limits, distinguished by whether pulse bandwidth and train length resolve the motional comb at δ = kωm. WP-Coastline v0.1.1 maps that boundary quantitatively.

Strong-binding / resolved-sideband limit

Criterion: 1/N ≪ 1 and δt/Tm ≪ 1 and η|α| < 1. Discrete comb at δ = kωm is resolved; each tooth carries ϑ₀-dependent spin coherence.

Observable signature: tooth visibility V = 1 − minϑ|C| approaches its Debye–Waller floor (Vimp ≈ 0.865 at η = 0.397). Hasse regime (N = 30, δt/Tm = 0.13, |α| ≤ 1) sits here.

Weak-binding / Doppler-merged limit

Criterion: pulse bandwidth 1/(ωmδt) or train bandwidth 1/N comparable to ωm, or Doppler halfwidth η|α|·ωm comparable to tooth spacing. Adjacent teeth merge into a Doppler-like continuum.

Observable signature: V collapses while off-tooth coherence P(δ = ½ωm) stays ≈ 1 (pulse-broadening) — or both drop (Doppler merging, not reached at this detuning). Discriminated by the two-map V/P rubric.

At the WP-V calibration (N = 30, δt/Tm = 0.13, η = 0.397, |α| = 3) the three bandwidth ratios are comparable — 1/N ≈ 0.03, 1/(ωmδt) ≈ 1.22, η|α| ≈ 1.19. The protocol is neither impulsive nor deeply resolved, which is why a quantitative coastline was worth producing rather than assumed.
