One-Sentence Claim

Hasse et al. phase-lock four oscillators (MW reference, optical running-wave phase, spin, motion) and use a stroboscopic Raman pulse train on a short-period travelling polarisation gradient (λeff ≈ 140 nm, ηLF ≈ 0.40) to map an ion's motional phase-space dynamics with super-resolution, where position appears as a spin-fringe phase shift and the p-quadrature proxy appears through the characteristic Doppler-detuning spectrum of the analysis pulse, encoding the ion's velocity distribution.

Sideband scaling ~ηs
Heuristic. Actual weights depend on n via Laguerre polynomials.

The Doppler Mechanism: Primary Momentum Readout

The analysis pulse is a two-photon Raman transition with keff = 2π/λeff. An ion moving with velocity v sees a Doppler-shifted transition:

δeff = δ0 + δD   where   δD = keff · v = (η / x0) · v

For a coherent state |α⟩ at ωm, the peak Doppler shift is:

δDpeak = 2η · ωm · |α|

The detuning spectrum P↑(δ) directly encodes the velocity distribution. Scanning the analysis-pulse frequency sweeps a narrow velocity filter across the distribution.

Analytic check

The σz contrast of 0.61 for the ground state is consistent with Doppler dephasing from zero-point motion: the zero-point Doppler width σDzp/(2π) = 0.52 MHz already exceeds Ω/(2π) = 0.3 MHz, so even at α = 0 the velocity spread partially washes out the σz fringe. The effective carrier Rabi rate in the settings (0.277 MHz = 0.3 × 0.924) independently confirms exp(−η²/2) ≈ 0.924.

α = 1 — ⟨n⟩ = 1

σz contrast rises to 0.71. Coherence peak broadens compared to α = 0 — the displaced ion's velocity distribution extends the Doppler envelope. The sideband pattern in σx, σy becomes asymmetric about δ = 0, reflecting the broken symmetry of the displaced state. Entropy broadens correspondingly.

α = 3 — ⟨n⟩ = 9

σz contrast reaches 0.84 — the highest in this series. Coherence broadens further, now spanning a wider detuning range. The sideband structure in σx, σy shows increasing complexity: more sidebands become populated as the displaced state's Fock-space support extends to higher n. The entropy distribution widens, consistent with Doppler broadening from peak velocities at δDpeak/Ω ≈ 10.

α = 5 — ⟨n⟩ = 25

σz contrast drops to 0.75 — the non-monotonic trend reverses. Coherence envelope is broadest, consistent with peak Doppler shift δDpeak/Ω ≈ 17. The sideband pattern in σx, σy is now dense with overlapping contributions from many Fock components. Entropy spreads across a wide detuning range.

Reading the Numerical Results

What the coherence envelope tells us

The coherence (Bloch vector length in the equatorial plane) directly reveals the Doppler velocity distribution. At each detuning δ0, only the velocity class satisfying keff·v ≈ −δ0 contributes coherently; other velocity classes dephase. The coherence envelope is therefore a proxy for the velocity distribution convolved with the Rabi instrument function. Its progressive broadening from α = 0 → 5 confirms the Doppler mechanism quantitatively.

σz contrast: non-monotonic behaviour

The σz contrast follows 0.61 → 0.71 → 0.84 → 0.75. This non-monotonic trend arises from the interplay of two effects. For the ground state (α = 0), even zero-point Doppler width (σD/Ω = 1.73) partially dephases the σz fringe. As α increases, the stroboscopic sampling catches the ion at different velocities across its oscillation cycle; the carrier coupling is modulated by the instantaneous Doppler shift, and the time-averaged σz signal reflects the overlap between the velocity distribution and the Rabi instrument function. The initial contrast rise from α = 0 → 3 and the subsequent drop at α = 5 encode information about the velocity distribution's shape — this is momentum information appearing directly in the population channel.

Sideband structure as Doppler fingerprint

The σx, σy traces show sideband oscillations with spacing ~ωm. As α increases, the sideband pattern becomes asymmetric and increasingly complex. In the sideband picture, this reflects higher-order transitions becoming significant as the Fock-space support broadens. In the Doppler picture, it reflects the frequency-dependent coupling of different velocity classes to the analysis pulse. The two descriptions are equivalent; the Doppler interpretation is more transparent for reading off momentum information.

Entropy as spin–motion entanglement map

The von Neumann entropy of the spin state (lower panels) peaks where spin–motion entanglement is strongest — at detunings where the analysis pulse partially drives sidebands, creating superpositions of spin-up-with-one-motional-state and spin-down-with-another. The entropy broadening with α maps the growing range of Doppler-accessible transitions. Far from resonance (|δ| ≫ ωm), entropy drops because the pulse is too far detuned to drive any transition.

Practical Starting Points

1. Reproduce the coherence envelope analytically

The simulation coherence profiles for α = 0, 1, 3, 5 are now available as benchmarks. Compute the stroboscopic average of the Rabi instrument function convolved with the time-dependent velocity distribution for each α. The coherence at detuning δ0 should be proportional to the overlap of the Rabi lineshape with the Doppler distribution at that offset. Matching the simulated envelope shape and width is the first quantitative test of the Doppler model (WP-A.1).

2. Explain the σz contrast trend

The non-monotonic sequence 0.61 → 0.71 → 0.84 → 0.75 is a concrete numerical target for WP-B. An analytic model that reproduces this trend from the velocity-distribution overlap with the Rabi lineshape, without free parameters, would close R5 and establish Doppler dominance definitively.

3. Coherent vs. thermal discrimination

Run the same simulation for a thermal state at n̄ = 9 (matching α = 3's ⟨n⟩). The coherence envelope should be symmetric and broader (σD/(2π) = 2.5 MHz vs. the coherent state's shifted but narrower distribution). This is the one-shot diagnostic that demonstrates the Doppler spectrum's discriminating power.

4. Sideband–Doppler consistency

Compute the detuning spectrum from the sideband picture (summing transitions to s = 0, 1, 2, 3 with Laguerre weights) and from the Doppler picture (velocity convolution) for α = 1. The two should converge to the simulated result. Discrepancies at the tails identify where truncation fails and where the full exponential coupling matters.
