One-Sentence Claim

Hasse et al. phase-lock four oscillators (MW reference, optical running-wave phase, spin, motion) and use a stroboscopic Raman pulse train on a short-period travelling polarisation gradient (λeff ≈ 140 nm, ηLF ≈ 0.40) to map an ion's motional phase-space dynamics with super-resolution, where position appears primarily as a spin-fringe phase shift and the p-quadrature proxy appears through the characteristic Doppler-detuning spectrum of the analysis pulse, encoding the ion's velocity distribution.

Glossary

Momentum observable
A velocity / p-quadrature proxy estimator inferred from the Doppler-detuning response of the stroboscopic analysis pulse, not a direct canonical P measurement.
Super-resolution
Position sensitivity at the scale of an engineered short-period polarisation gradient (λeff ≈ 140 nm ≪ optical wavelength). Does not imply sub-x0 or QND.
Sideband scaling ~ηs
Heuristic mnemonic. Actual matrix elements depend on n through Laguerre polynomial structure.
Detuning spectrum
Spin-flip probability P↑(δ) as function of analysis-pulse detuning. For a moving ion, δ includes the Doppler shift keff·v, so this spectrum directly encodes the velocity distribution.

Interaction Hamiltonian

For one motional mode (Supplement):

HTI = ℏ(ωz/2)σz + ℏωm a†a + ℏ(Ω/2)[C σ− + C† σ+]

where   C(η, a, a†) = exp(iη(a + a†))

The coupling is an exponential in the position quadrature — not truncated. This is the source of both power (Doppler-resolved momentum mapping, higher sidebands) and complications (backaction, nonlinear decoding).

Where η ≈ 0.4 Enters

AC Raman: λeff ≈ 140 nm, ηLF ≈ 0.40, ηMF ≈ 0.23, ηHF ≈ 0.18. MW and CC: η ≈ 0. Not in the deep Lamb–Dicke regime.

Sideband content (from |0⟩)

Debye–Waller exp(−η²/2) ≈ 0.923. Matrix elements |⟨s|C|0⟩|: carrier 0.923, 1st 0.369, 2nd 0.074, 3rd 0.010. Higher-n transitions involve Laguerre polynomials.

Measurement Mechanism: Two Channels

Position → phase shift

Travelling wave imprints spin-dependent phase ∝ keff·x(t).

Velocity → Doppler detuning

An ion with velocity v sees a Doppler-shifted two-photon transition: δD = keff·v = (η/x0)·v. For coherent states, the peak Doppler shift is 2η·ωm·|α|. At the paper's parameters this exceeds Ω for all α ≥ 1, making the detuning spectrum the primary momentum channel.

Semiclassical Baseline

⟨α|exp(iη(a+a†))|α⟩ = exp(−η²/2) · exp(i 2η Re α)

Debye–Waller factor + phase ∝ displacement quadrature. Deviations quantify backaction/nonclassicality.

Backaction Structure

True BAE requires linear coupling to one rotating quadrature; exp(iη(a+a†)) at η ≈ 0.4 couples both. Open question whether a perturbative regime exists (L6).

Tomographic Potential

Phase shift (position) and Doppler spectrum (momentum) together access the full phase-space quasi-probability distribution. Scanning analysis-pulse frequency traces the velocity distribution at each oscillation phase. Characteristic function sampling ⟨exp(ik x)⟩ provides a complementary formalism.

The Doppler Mechanism: Primary Momentum Readout

The analysis pulse is a two-photon Raman transition driven by the AC travelling wave with keff = 2π/λeff. An ion moving with velocity v along keff sees a Doppler-shifted transition. The effective detuning becomes:

δeff = δ0 + δD   where   δD = keff · v = (η / x0) · v

For a coherent state |α⟩ oscillating at ωm, the velocity at the turning point is vpeak = 2x0ωm|α|, giving a peak Doppler shift:

δDpeak = 2η · ωm · |α|

The spin-flip probability as a function of analysis-pulse frequency — the detuning spectrum — directly encodes the velocity distribution. Scanning this frequency (or equivalently, sampling at different motional phases where velocity differs) traces the distribution. The centre gives mean velocity; the width gives velocity spread — zero-point for coherent states, broader for thermal or squeezed states.

The Detuning Spectrum

For an analysis pulse of duration τ = π/(2Ω) (nominal π/2) and effective detuning δ:

P↑(δ) = (Ω² / Ωeff²) · sin²(Ωeff τ / 2)   where   Ωeff = √(Ω² + δ²)

This Rabi lineshape is the instrument function for velocity measurement. Its width is set by Ω; velocities producing δD ≫ Ω are strongly suppressed:

At α = 1, the ion at peak velocity is essentially invisible to a resonant analysis pulse (P↑ drops from 0.50 to 0.007). The pulse acts as a narrow velocity filter centred on v = 0 with width ~Ω/keff. Scanning the pulse frequency sweeps this filter across the velocity distribution.

From detuning spectrum to momentum distribution

For a motional state with velocity distribution P(v), the observed spin-flip probability at set detuning δ0 is the convolution:

⟨P↑⟩(δ0) = ∫ P(v) · P↑(δ0 + keffv) dv

Standard deconvolution: the instrument function P↑(δ) has known shape (set by Ω, τ); the unknown is P(v). For a coherent state |α⟩ sampled at a specific oscillation phase, the velocity distribution is Gaussian with mean v̄ = 2x0ωm|α| sin(ωmt) and width σv = x0ωm (the zero-point spread, independent of α).

Doppler width for thermal states

For a thermal state at mean occupation n̄, the velocity distribution is Gaussian with width σv = x0ωm√(2n̄+1), giving Doppler width σD/(2π) = ηωm√(2n̄+1)/(2π):

Sideband ↔ Doppler equivalence

In the Lamb–Dicke limit, the first-order coupling iη(a+a†)σ− drives transitions |↓,n⟩ → |↑,n±1⟩ split by ωm from the carrier — the discrete sideband picture. The Doppler picture is the semiclassical equivalent: velocity v produces a continuous shift keff·v, and discrete sidebands emerge because the velocity oscillates at ωm. The two descriptions are fully equivalent for small η. At η = 0.4, the sideband picture requires summing to s ≥ 2 for quantitative accuracy; the Doppler picture absorbs all orders into a single detuning parameter, which is why it provides cleaner analytic estimates for coherent-state dynamics.

Contrast Loss as Integrated Doppler Effect

The earlier briefing described "momentum via contrast modulation from finite-pulse-time averaging." This is a secondary description of the same physics. During a flash, the ion traverses a range of velocities; averaging P↑ over the resulting Doppler shifts reduces fringe visibility. Contrast loss is the frequency-integrated effect of the Doppler mechanism — it discards the spectral information that a full detuning scan preserves.

For continuing research, the detuning spectrum is the more informative observable. Contrast loss remains a quick diagnostic (it signals when Doppler broadening is significant) but is not the primary measurement channel for momentum or state reconstruction.

Practical Starting Points

1. First calculation: Doppler lineshape for a coherent state

For |α⟩ sampled at ωmt = π/2 (max velocity), compute ⟨P↑⟩(δ0) = ∫ G(v; v̄, σv) · P↑(δ0 + keffv) dv for α = 0, 1, 3. You should see: a centred peak at α = 0, a shifted peak at α = 1 (by 1.04 MHz), and a strongly shifted and flattened peak at α = 3 (by 3.12 MHz). Width encodes zero-point velocity spread in all cases.

2. Coherent vs. thermal: a one-shot diagnostic

Compare Doppler lineshape of |α = √5⟩ (⟨n⟩ = 5) with thermal n̄ = 5. The coherent state at fixed oscillation phase has a narrow Gaussian (σD/(2π) = 0.52 MHz) shifted from zero; the thermal state has a broad Gaussian (1.72 MHz) centred at zero. Trivially distinguishable from a single detuning scan.

3. Numbers to reproduce from the paper

Verify δD/Ω ratios above using Ω/(2π) = 0.3 MHz, ηLF = 0.40, ωLF/(2π) = 1.30 MHz. Then estimate keffΔx during one flash for |α| = 1 (requires δt from supplement). This connects Doppler and geometric pictures: keffΔx ≈ δD·δt.

4. Small-η validation ladder

Compute the detuning spectrum at each truncation order (linear, quadratic, full exponential) for α = 1. Linear should capture peak shift but miss tails. Quadratic brings tails within ~3%. This validates the truncation guidance and establishes which order is needed per observable.

5. Simulation scaffold for WP-C

Use QuTiP: construct M: ρ → (phase, Doppler spectrum) for displaced thermal, Fock, squeezed, cat states. For each, record both channels. Ask: can you recover ρ from combined data? The two channels together provide richer information than contrast loss alone — the Doppler spectrum resolves velocity structure that contrast merely averages over.
